# Desktop Specific Generations - What to Look For

## Lenovo ThinkCentre

### M75s Gen 5 (Current/Recent)
- **Model Numbers**: 21D8, 21D9, 21DA
- **CPU**: AMD Ryzen 7 PRO 5750GE or Intel i7-13700
- **Released**: 2023-2024
- **Look for**: "M75s Gen 5" or "M75s (5th Gen)"

### M75q Gen 5 (Tiny Form Factor)
- **Model Numbers**: Similar to M75s
- **CPU**: Same as M75s
- **Released**: 2023-2024
- **Look for**: "M75q Gen 5" (q = tiny, s = small form factor)

### Older Generations (Still Good):
- **M75s Gen 4**: 2022-2023, still good performance
- **M75s Gen 3**: 2021-2022, older but cheaper

**Recommendation**: Look for **Gen 5** (newest) or **Gen 4** (still great, might be cheaper)

## How to Identify Generation

### On Product Page:
- **Lenovo**: "ThinkCentre M75s Gen 5" or "M75s (5th Gen)"
- **HP**: "EliteDesk 805 G9" (G9 = generation 9)
- **Dell**: "OptiPlex 7010" (7010 = model number, indicates generation)

### In Model Number:
- **Lenovo**: 21D8, 21D9 = Gen 5
- **HP**: 805 G9 = Generation 9
- **Dell**: 7010 = Current generation

### CPU as Indicator:
- **2024 models**: Intel i7-13700, i7-14700, AMD Ryzen 7 PRO 5750GE
- **2023 models**: Intel i7-13700, AMD Ryzen 7 PRO 5750G
- **2022 models**: Intel i7-12700, AMD Ryzen 7 PRO 5750G

---

## What Generation to Buy

### Best Value: Current Generation (Gen 5, G9, 7010)
- **Pros**: Latest CPUs, best performance, newest features
- **Cons**: More expensive
- **When**: If you want the best performance

### Good Value: Previous Generation (Gen 4, G8, 7000)
- **Pros**: Still great performance, often 20-30% cheaper
- **Cons**: Slightly older CPUs
- **When**: If you want to save money

### Avoid: Older Than 2 Generations
- **Why**: Much older CPUs, less performance
- **When**: Only if budget is very tight
