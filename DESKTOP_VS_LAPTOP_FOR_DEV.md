# Desktop vs Laptop for Development

## Why Desktop is Better

### Pros:
- ✅ **Much cheaper** - Same specs cost 30-50% less
- ✅ **More powerful** - Better CPUs, more RAM options
- ✅ **Easier to upgrade** - Add RAM, storage, GPU later
- ✅ **Better cooling** - No thermal throttling
- ✅ **More ports** - Easier to connect multiple monitors
- ✅ **Better for long sessions** - No battery concerns

### Cons:
- ❌ **Not portable** - Stuck at one location
- ❌ **Takes up space** - Need desk/monitor/keyboard/mouse
- ❌ **More setup** - Need to buy monitor, keyboard, mouse separately

## Desktop Options

### Option 1: Pre-Built Desktop (~$800-1,200)
**Lenovo ThinkCentre, HP EliteDesk, Dell OptiPlex:**
- CPU: Intel i5/i7 or AMD Ryzen 5/7
- RAM: 16-32GB (often upgradeable)
- Storage: 512GB-1TB SSD
- **Much cheaper than laptop with same specs**

### Option 2: Build Your Own (~$600-1,000)
**Custom build:**
- CPU: AMD Ryzen 5 5600 or Intel i5-12400
- RAM: 32GB DDR4 (cheap!)
- Storage: 1TB NVMe SSD
- **Best value, most upgradeable**

### Option 3: Mini PC (~$500-800)
**Small form factor:**
- Intel NUC, ASUS Mini PC, etc.
- Good for small spaces
- Still powerful enough for dev work

## Desktop Specs for Dev Work

**Minimum:**
- CPU: Intel i5 or AMD Ryzen 5 (6+ cores)
- RAM: 16GB (upgradeable to 32GB)
- Storage: 512GB SSD
- **Cost: ~$600-800**

**Recommended:**
- CPU: Intel i7 or AMD Ryzen 7 (8+ cores)
- RAM: 32GB (plenty of headroom)
- Storage: 1TB SSD
- **Cost: ~$800-1,200**

**"Destroy Everything" Tier:**
- CPU: Intel i7/i9 or AMD Ryzen 7/9
- RAM: 32-64GB
- Storage: 1TB+ SSD
- **Cost: ~$1,000-1,500**
